Projections

Here I have a list of all of my projections with descriptions and examples.

Equirectangular

A simple linear mapping of latitude vs longitude.

Mercator

An extremely common shape-preserving cylindrical map.

Gall Stereographic

A compromising cylindrical map.

Cyllindrical Equal-Area

An equal-area cylindrical projection.

Polar

An equidistant azimuthal projection.

Stereographic

A shape-preserving azimuthal map.

Azimuthal Equal-Area

An equal-area azimuthal projection.

Orthographic

A realistic rendering of the Earth viewed from a great distance.

Gnomonic

A projection where all great circles are straight lines.

Winkel Tripel

A compromise projection used by National Geographic (not currently functional).

Van der Grinten

A round map projection similar to Mercator.

Mollweide

An equal-area projection shaped like an ellipse.

Hammer

Another equal-area projection shaped like an ellipse.

Sinusoidal

An equal-area projection shaped like a sinusoid.

Lemons

The earth unfolded into a bunch of little lemon-shaped things.

Pierce Quincuncial

A complex shape-preserving map shaped like a square.

Guyou Hemisphere-in-a-Square

Pierce Quincuncial, but rearranged a little bit.

AuthaGraph

A nearly-equal-area tetrahedral projection.

Tetragraph

A compromising tetrahedral projection that I invented after being inspired by the AuthaGraph.
